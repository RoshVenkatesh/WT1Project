Put the fish in the fishbowl

Stare at the yellow stripes on the fish in the picture below
for about 10-20 sec.
Then move your gaze to the fish bowl.

A fish of a different color is seen in the bowl
You just saw an after-image In the retina of your eyes, there are three types of color receptors (cones)
that are most sensitive to either red, blue or green.
When you stare at a particular color for too long, these receptors get fatigued.
When you then look at a different background, the receptors that are tired do not work as well.
Therefore, the information from all of the different color receptors is not in balance.
This will create the color afterimages.
